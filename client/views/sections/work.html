Just before I started my period as a Freelancer, I had the grate opportunity to work at Amadeus A huge company that develops worldwide products. The biggest GDS of the world with multiple products where the reusability of code is essential.
Millions of lines of code, where the mastery of dessign is a requirement for the propper mainteinance of the software.
Releases of multiple Change Requests where without a very robust and stric process of Integration and QA, everything would just blow up without any possible way to keep this propertly functioning.

But at some point I´ve seen I´d rather create a simple thing from scratch that belonging to something already complex and big. So much big that it is very complicated to participate in real processes of creativity.

Anyway, those almost 3 years gave me the oportunity to live in the world of the gigants, and that provided me an expertise that has a grate value for me now:

  • DEV phases are the best chance to make things good, to create good code. You just touch things locally and commit to a development stream shared by just a few.
  • Because everything becomes much more expensive at the INT phase. Most of all when decens of teams are touching the same functionallity.
  • There are programmed builds, things become less agile. The methodology becomes heavier.
  • A build that can be broken!. Yes, you can brake the build. And you can receive that mail you don´t want to see in your inbox a Friday morning.
  • Or the others can brake it and you´ll might need to wait until the INT server builds at noon or tonight to test your code.
  • Understand that is not just about being in time to deliver for DEV INT and QA phases. But to do things well done, because everything you missed, it will came back in a form of a Severity Hight indidence to your team´s inbox, and you wil have to deal with something that is not working, but this time in a much much worst server...in a production server!

I had the chance to learn everything behind the processes and methodologies of an enormous company and understand the why of all of it as well the tools needed. I had the opportunity to work in a multicultural environment with decens of nationalities and learn fluently my third language. Improve my comunication skills, get use to make presentations, write documentation and so much more...

Daniboomerang

What you´re seing right now!. It´s a pure CSS parallax able to simulate 4 levels of profoundity. The cloud, the world of the internet. A way to show the technologies, frameworks and programing languages I know. Pretends to be my CV.
But I´ve created daniboomerang for several reasons:

  • The main one was to have something where I could clearly show what I´m capable of and how do I do things. You can install the app locally and do whatever pleases you.
  • But I also had a challenge since a long time ago: to do something I´d love to see from the Graphyc Designing and UX point of view. Also create my own images.
  • I wanted something fully responsive. From 200px up to TVs. Something alive that understand that you are using a very small screen and things need to be arranged in a different way.
  • I wanted something completelly tested. With plenty of tests, all of them passed.
  • A Continuous Integration server
  • Achive grate performance: with over 30 SVG images simulating parallax, changing the background position with JS wasn´t possible. Using a pure CSS parallax solution and optimizing images was the ony way to reach a smooth scroll (Over 30 images < 6MB)

It is a MEAN app for the management and administration of incidences and inventory for a net of schools where I had to lead with:
