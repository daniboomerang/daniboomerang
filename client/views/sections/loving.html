Loving

My heart is always been at the back-end side. I´ve always been amazed about design patterns and the art of making growing software modular and scalable. I never had any special feeling about the UI side.

And then, a few years ago, a huge technological revolution has changed things, right?.
The world has reinvented itself, with all kind of screen sizes moving arround, and all connected by a cloud that has inclredibly grown in capabilities.
So many kind of devices, so many new concepts to be created.
Reaching a point that the art of UX has been inevitably stablished and web development has become something with tremendous potential. With plenty of new frameworks that provide us a way to reach this potential.

We all have had very nice ideas. And enjoy searching for them. But sooner or later everyone learns that there is no idea without its execution. So a couple of years ago I decided I´d love to be able to execute anyting could came to my mind. And so I decided to learn the Full-Stack. And this is what I enjoy today and I am focused doing it with MEAN.

And of course everything changes and I am sure there are plenty of projects that have nothing to do with MEAN that I´d love to be part of, so I am all ears. But this is what I love to do today:

  • I enjoy with AngularJS especially now that I´ve gone deep into the framework, now that I completely understand the pourpose its components, when to use what and when not to. And even more important: to see its limitations.
  • I admire their decision for such a radical change at AngularJS 2, getting rid of so many basic components and concepts of the first framework. I think it´s what was needed and I am looking forward to give it a shot.
  • I like pure REST APIs architecture for CRUD apps and I enjoy developing them with Express.
  • I am pleased by storing data rich structures in MongoDB without actually caring about what and how those structures are.
  • And although I´ll always love OOP, I have to admit that it is kind of cool developing with NodeJS a Back-end that works concurrenty and in the same language as my Front-end.

And I don´t remember my self to enjoy creating software as I do it right now, and that´s why I am sure I´ll be a grate asset.
